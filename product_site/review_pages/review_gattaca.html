Gattaca

Gattaca is a 1997 science fiction film directed by Andrew Niccol, set in a near-future society driven by genetic engineering. The story follows Vincent Freeman, a man conceived naturally in a world where genetic perfection determines social status and career opportunities. Labeled as "in-valid" due to his natural birth, Vincent dreams of traveling into space but faces systemic discrimination. To achieve his goal, he assumes the identity of a genetically superior individual, navigating a complex web of deception, surveillance, and moral dilemmas. Gattaca explores themes of ambition, identity, discrimination, and the ethical implications of genetic manipulation, offering a thought-provoking vision of a society obsessed with perfection.
