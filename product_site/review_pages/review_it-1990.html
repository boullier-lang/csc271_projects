It (1990)

It (1990) is a horror television miniseries based on Stephen King's 1986 novel of the same name. Set in the small town of Derry, Maine, the story follows a group of children who are terrorized by a malevolent, shape-shifting entity that primarily appears as Pennywise the Dancing Clown. The children, who call themselves the Losers’ Club, band together to confront their deepest fears and uncover the dark secrets lurking beneath their seemingly quiet town. Decades later, the creature resurfaces, forcing the now-adult members of the group to face it again and confront both the horrors of their past and the evil that continues to haunt Derry. It (1990) is widely recognized for its chilling atmosphere, iconic villain, and exploration of childhood trauma, friendship, and courage in the face of unimaginable fear.

As a longtime horror fan, I can confidently say that It (1990) is a chilling classic that still holds up decades later. The story of Pennywise and the Losers’ Club perfectly blends suspense, childhood fears, and small-town dread. Tim Curry’s performance as Pennywise is iconic and terrifying, making every scene with him unforgettable. While some of the special effects are dated, the storytelling and atmosphere more than make up for it. It (1990) earns a solid 9/10 for its unforgettable characters and enduring scares.


UserB (7/10) [TV Miniseries Watcher, Horror Newcomer, Suspense Lover]
Watching It (1990) for the first time, I can see why it’s considered a horror classic. The tension builds slowly but effectively, and the interactions between the Losers’ Club feel genuine and heartfelt. That said, some of the pacing drags in the middle, and the effects can feel cheesy to modern viewers. Despite that, Pennywise is genuinely scary, and the story’s themes of friendship and facing fears resonate well. Overall, I’d give it a 7/10 — scary, memorable, and emotionally engaging, though a bit dated.
